I3DA: Immersive Media in Heritage, Nature and Consciousness

I3DA 2026 will again focus on innovation in 3D immersive audio, including virtual acoustics, architectural and musical acoustics, subjective evaluations and listening tests, immersive soundscape design, and creative uses of immersive technologies. Furthermore, I3DA 2026 will be a special edition, that will include visual and interactive aspects of immersive media, such as related computer graphics, LIDAR scans, photogrammetry, immersive world building, digital archaeology, 3D visualization, and hardware and software applications across both fields. The aim is to open new possibilities for collaboration, networking, and deeper understanding between complementary sensory domains in immersive media.

I3DA 2026 would like to involve academic and industrial researchers to share their latest research, methods, and demos related to Immersive and 3D Audio, Interaction Design and Visuals in Audio Engineering, Computer Science and Visuals. Topics include virtual/augmented reality and their applications, digital archaeology, musical acoustics, signal processing in acoustics, non-linear acoustics in auralisation, machine learning related to immersive media, acoustics, heritage acoustics, psychoacoustics, gaming, art and sound studies contexts, bioacoustics and the environment. Further topics may be accepted.

I3DA will be indexed on IEEE like the last years, with Special Issues like last year.

To submit your full paper, please log into your SUBMISSION, click ADD FILES on the top-right hand side and add your file. The papers must be submitted via EasyChair paper management system, following the IEEE guidelines for proceedings papers.

Collaboration with Fondazione Levi

23 December 2025

We are pleased to announce a collaboration with the Fondazione Levi. On this special occasion of the International Conference in Immersive and 3D Audio, both the spaces of Fondazione Levi and those of Warwick University at Palazzo Giustinian Lolin, on the Grand Canal, will be made available for our event.

Fondazione Ugo e Olga Levi, founded in Venice in 1962 and based in the historic Palazzo Giustinian Lolin on the Grand Canal, is an international centre for musical and musicological research. It preserves, studies, and promotes the tangible and intangible heritage of music through its specialised library, research projects, and cultural initiatives in Italy and abroad. Alongside its scholarly activities, the Foundation promotes the dissemination of musical knowledge through a significant publishing programme devoted to academic publications, scholarly series, and musicological editions. Committed to the education of new generations, it engages primary and secondary school students, university students, doctoral candidates, and researchers through scholarships, internships, traineeships, and educational projects.
